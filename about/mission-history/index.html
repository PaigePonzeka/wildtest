Mission & History

The wild project is a theater, film, music, and visual arts venue that presents diverse, engaging, inspiring, and entertaining works to the vibrant and multi-cultural community of (omitted Alphabet City) New York’s East Village, while bringing together the artists and the environment in a unique way. Founded in 2007, the wild project is an innovator among arts venues, providing an eco-friendly theater and gallery where the artists and space nurture each other. The venue is dedicated to engendering an atmosphere that supports the artists, and to cultivating artists who are committed to artistic excellence, enrich the community and promote social justice.(Note: could change social justice to inclusion or awareness. The aim is to make the sentence broader, as now it sounds like WP only presents productions about the environment) With an eco-conscious approach to presenting the dynamic works of hundreds of distinct (Added) and emerging artists each year, the wild project offers a creative and inclusive venue(could change inclusive to accessible) for patrons of all ages, interests, and incomes in its community.
